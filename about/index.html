About Me

I have been participating in the "gig economy" since high school through tutoring English, transcribing and translating (VN-EN) consumer interviews, and facilitating group discussions at the HCMC English Speaking Club. My first professional job (albeit still part-time) was with the International Student and Faculty Services (now Office of International Services) at Oregon State University, which lasted for five years. After that, I worked for OSU Statistics department, teaching lab and recitation sections in undergraduate courses, mostly ST 352 and ST 201.

Education-wise, I studied economics and statistics at Oregon State University. My current interests are in research methods, data visualization, and data analysis techniques. You can find some of my past projects on this site.
